
# Social Media Professionalism Checklist

### 5 Do's
1. **Optimize your bio:** Use a professional headshot and a clear description of your skills (e.g., "B.Tech Student | Python Developer").
2. **Share your journey:** Post about hackathons you attend or projects you are building to build a "proof of work."
3. **Engage meaningfully:** Comment on industry leaders' posts with insightful questions or thoughts.
4. **Fact-check before sharing:** Ensure any technical or news-related content you share is accurate.
5. **Use Privacy Settings:** Regularly review who can see your personal posts versus your professional ones.

### 5 Don'ts
1. **Avoid "Doom-scrolling" or oversharing:** Keep personal grievances or highly controversial topics off professional platforms.
2. **Don't ignore DMs:** If a recruiter or peer reaches out, try to respond within 24–48 hours.
3. **Never plagiarize:** Always give credit when sharing someone else's code, design, or article.
4. **Don't use unprofessional handles:** Avoid usernames like "GamerBoy123" for professional LinkedIn or GitHub accounts.
5. **Don't post in the heat of the moment:** If you're frustrated with a project or a grade, wait until you've cooled down before posting.
